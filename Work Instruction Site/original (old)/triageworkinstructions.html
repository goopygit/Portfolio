"Quick" Triage Work Instructions (JoyCon Swap Only Process)

Precautions/Preparation

▹ All operators must be wearing gloves.
▹ All benches must have scratch protection in the form of either pink foam or the ESD mat.
▹ All operators must follow one piece flow, meaning there is only one repair order on the bench at a time.
There are no exceptions to these rules. If there is a preventative, inform a lead or supervisor immediately.

Step 1: Validate All Information


The operator should validate all information.

▹ Each RO on the box label should correspond to a work order in the box.
If any do not match completely, are missing, or have duplicates, this is a failure and must be processed as a discrepancy.



▹ All Joycons in the box must correspond to the items received on the work order tag and written on the traveler.
If there is more than one joycon, each joycon must have a corresponding work order.

▹ All paperwork in the box must belong to the same consumer at the same address.
If there is any address mismatch, this must be processed as a discrepancy.

Step 2: Process The Exchange

During the swap-only period, symptom verification is not required.
All joycons must be exchanged.
To perform the exchange, use the Quick Triage Page.
This process must be performed for each individual joycon/RO.

▹ First, scan the RO into the box labeled RO number.
▹ Next, hit "Enter" on the keyboard, or click "Look Up RO". You should receive a message stating that this process was successful:

If you receive an error, fail this RO as a discrepancy and give it to a team lead or supervisor.

▹ Next, The form will populate the consumer name and address. Once again, compare this to the work order in front of you to ensure you are working on the correct RO.
▹ If all information matches, proceed with entering your initials:

▹ After entering your initials, click the Part Usage button:

▹ A new window will open. Click confirm in order to activate the form.

You must enter a part number, the quantity (this should always be 1), and your initials. If you need a part number reference, ask a team lead or supervisor.

▹ When the form is filled out, click add part. It should populate the list with the part you added.

▹ Click Save Part Usage, and click Yes to confirm.

▹ You may now close the part usage window, and finish the process by clicking "Update Order" on the Quick Triage Entry page.


This completes the entry process in the computer, but the task is not yet complete.

Step 3: Replace the JoyCon

▹ Label each defective joycon with the RO Number on masking tape using a marker.
Pen is not acceptable for writing the RO number on the JoyCon.
Mark and initial the joycon traveler in the Triage and QC sections. The QC processes have already been completed by the operator who cleaned and wrapped it.

▹ Place the defective joycon into a box of cores labeled with your initials and the date.
▹ Take special care to replace it with the correct joycon. This should match the incoming joycon, as well as the part number you assigned to the RO.

This completes the repair order for triage! Return all items and paperwork to the correct box, and put it on your outgoing work cart to be given to write-up.
